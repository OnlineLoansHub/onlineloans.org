I Only Made $5K This Month — Can I Still Get a Business Loan?

Hey, I run a small online store and I made around $5,000 this month. I don’t have perfect credit and I’m not a big company. Is it even possible to get approved for a business loan or line of credit? Or is this stuff only for high-revenue businesses?

Absolutely — you don’t need to be a huge business or earn six figures monthly to get approved for funding. Many lenders today specialize in helping small and growing businesses with modest monthly revenue.

If you’re making at least $4,000–$5,000/month consistently and have been in business for 3–6 months, you may qualify for:

  • A business line of credit (flexible, reuse funds as needed)
  • A short-term working capital loan
  • Or even revenue-based financing (repayment tied to your sales)

Your credit score matters, but it’s not everything — lenders look at your bank statements, deposits, and consistency. Even if your credit is below 650, some online lenders will work with you (just expect slightly higher rates).

Pro Tip:
Improve your odds by:

  • Keeping your business and personal accounts separate
  • Showing clean, consistent deposits
  • Preparing 3–6 months of statements before applying

You’d be surprised how many small business owners qualify once they apply — even with lower revenue or imperfect credit.
